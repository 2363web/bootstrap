We made great strides in 2009, competeing in two regional competitions in 2009: the New Jersey Regional and the NASA/VCU Regional. We finished the New Jersey Regional with 4 wins and 3 losses- 37th place out of 61 teams. At the 2009 NASA/VCU Regional, we finished with 5 wins and 2 losses- 15th place out of 60 teams, Unfortunately, no team selected us to partner with them for the elimination matches, and we were poised to go home disappointed. Hard work paid off, however, as the team won the Underwriters' Laboratory Industrial Safety Award.

In 2010, we competed at the Washington DC and Virginia Regionals. We finished the Washington DC Regional with 7 wins and 2 losses, but were not selected to compete in the elimination matches. However, we continued our winning streak by receiving the Underwriters' Laboratory Industrial Safety Award for the second year in a row. At the Virginia Regional, we once again performed well and were finally selected by the number 8 alliance as a competitor for the elimination matches. The alliance was eliminated in the quarter-final matches, but we had broken through and finally made it into the elimination rounds! We capped off the competition by receiving the Motorola Quality Award as the judges recognized the quality build of our, Genome Beta.

In the 2011 FRC season, we built Genome Gamma to play the game Logomotion. We competed in the Palmetto Regional and won the Engineering Excellence award. We also competed in the Virginia Regional, making it to finals and winning the Industrial design award. Our head coach Matt Wilbur was awarded one of the most prestigious awards in FIRST, the Woodie Flowers award. We made a great showing at the St. Louis World Championship, advancing to the quarter finals.

In March of 2012, we were invited to present and demonstrate our robot at a city wide STEM Summit, which focused on the new integrated STEM aspects in the classroom curriculum. The team's robot demo was used to highlight the curriculum used in robot design. In 2012, the team supported the creation of the FLL team Bricks 'N Bots, ran by a private group. We supported the team with robot kits, programming and mechanical instructions, and grants for them to travel and compete.
