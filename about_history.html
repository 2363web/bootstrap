The team made great strides in 2009, competeing in two regional competitions in 2009 -- the New Jersey Regional and the NASA/VCU Regional. The team finished the New Jersey Regional with a record of 4 wins and 3 losses in 37th place out of 61 teams. At the 2009 NASA/VCU Regional, the team finished with a 5 wins and 2 losses record in 15th place out of 60 teams, following the qualification matches. Unfortunately, no team selected us to partner with them for the elimination matches, and we were poised to go home disappointed. Hard work once again paid off, however, as the team won the Underwriters' Laboratory Industiral Safety Award.
In 2010, the team competed at the Washington DC Regional and the Virginia Regional held in Richmond, Virginia. The team finished the Washington DC Regional with a record of 7 wins and 2 losses, but was not selected to compete in the elimination matches. The team, however, continued its winning streak by receiving the Underwriters' Laboratory Industrial Safety Award for the second year in a row. At the Virginia Regional, the team once again performed well and was finally selected as a competitor for the elimination matches, being selected by the number 8 alliance. The alliance was eliminated in the quarter-final matches, but the team had broken through and finally made it into the elimination rounds! The competition was capped off by receiving the Motorola Quality Award as the judges recognized the quality build of the Triple Helix robot, Genome Beta.
In the 2011 FIRST Robotics Competition season, the team built Genome Gamma to play the game Logomotion. The team competed in the Palmetto Regional at the North Charleston Coliseum in North Charleston, South Carolina on March 24 - 26, 2011 and won the Engineering Excellence award and the event. The team also competed in the Virginia Regional in Richmond, Virginia on April 7 - 9, 2011 making it to finals and won the Industrial design award. Our head coach Matt Wilbur won the Woodie Flowers award. The team made a great showing at the St. Louis World Championship and advanced to the quarter finals.
In March of 2012, team 2363 was invited to present and demonstrate the robot at a city wide STEM Summit, which focused on the new integrated STEM aspects in the classroom curriculum. The team's robot demo was used to highlight the curriculum used in robotic design. In 2012, the team supported the creation of the FLL team Bricks 'N Bots, ran by a private group. They supported the teams with robot kits and provided them with programming and mechanical instructions, as well as grants for them to travel and compete.
